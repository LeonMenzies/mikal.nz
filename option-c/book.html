The Book

Time Management:
A Complete Waste of Time

Everything you have been told about managing time is wrong. This book explains why - and shows you a better way to live.

About the Book

A Challenge to Everything You Think You Know

For decades, the productivity industry has been telling us to plan better, work faster, use smarter systems and fill our calendars with greater precision. And yet most people feel more overwhelmed, more behind and less fulfilled than ever. Why?

Because the premise is wrong. Time cannot actually be managed. What can be managed - what must be managed - is you. Your energy, your focus, your priorities and your relationship with purpose. This is what Mikal Nielsen's book addresses with clarity, honesty and a healthy dose of provocation.

Written with a foreword by Ben Gioia, this is not a book full of tips and tricks. It is a book that challenges the assumptions beneath your habits - and gives you a genuinely better framework for living.

Inside the Book

What You Will Discover

This book does not ask you to be more disciplined. It asks you to be more honest. Honest about what you actually value, what is actually driving your choices and what kind of life you actually want to live.

By the time you finish it, you will have a completely different relationship with time - not because you have better tools, but because you have better understanding.

Six Core Insights

What the Book Gives You

Chapter Focus

Debunk the Myths

Seven deeply held beliefs about time management are examined and dismantled. These are not strawman arguments - they are things that genuinely shape how most people approach their days. Seeing them clearly is the first step to freedom from them.

Core Framework

The Life Mastery Framework

Mikal's proprietary framework gives you a structured way to think about all the dimensions of your life simultaneously. Rather than optimising one area at the expense of others, it shows you how to move everything forward together.

Deeper Purpose

Purposeful Living

Most productivity is in service of goals that were never examined. This book asks the harder question: what is it all for? And it gives you practical tools for answering that question with genuine clarity - not platitudes.

Inner Life

Inner Peace as a Practice

Peace is not the absence of pressure. It is the capacity to meet pressure without being undone by it. This book explores how to build that capacity - not through denial or detachment, but through genuine mental fitness.
