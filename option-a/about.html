Who is Mikal?

Meet Mikal Nielsen

Danish-born, earth citizen, 30+ years of coaching — and still deeply curious about what makes human beings flourish.

The Family Perspective

"Dad's weird!"

That is what Mikal's kids say — and they are probably right. He is passionate about two things above everything else: his own growth, and helping others grow. He is relentlessly curious about how things work — people, systems, the quiet mechanics of a fulfilled life.

Born in Denmark, Mikal became an earth citizen long before that was a fashionable concept. He met his New Zealand wife while travelling, and the two of them have built a life rooted in Northland — and a perspective shaped by over 50 countries.

He is a husband, a dad, a cafe-goer, a kitchen experimenter, a barefoot runner, and a skier who still gets excited about fresh snow. He also happens to have one of the most quietly transformative coaching practices in New Zealand.

The Turning Point

A life-changing experience at 27

Mikal's coaching journey didn't begin in a university lecture theatre. It began with a profound personal experience at the age of 27 — one that cracked open his understanding of what human beings are truly capable of.

Before that, he was shy. Bullied as a child. Someone who shrank rather than stood tall. The transformation from that boy to the confident, grounded speaker and coach he is today is not a story of willpower — it's a story of genuine insight.

That is the kind of change Mikal helps his clients create: not surface-level adjustment, but something that shifts the foundations.

"A revolution in thinking and approach to life, business and intimacy is desperately needed. Most people are living a fraction of what is possible — and they don't even know it." Mikal Nielsen
What Drives Him

Passionate about growth — his own and yours

Mikal's two deepest passions are his own continued growth and helping others discover theirs. He is not interested in patching problems. He is interested in what is underneath them — the beliefs, patterns, and blind spots that keep intelligent people stuck.

His 30-year coaching career has taken him across industries, continents, and life stages. He has worked with business owners and CEOs, with parents and partners, with people who have everything on paper and feel nothing inside.

He has been on national television and radio, featured in newspapers, and has spoken to audiences across New Zealand. He has written 4 books — 2 published independently, and 2 co-authored with his wife.

Beyond Coaching

The man behind the work

Mikal practises what he preaches. He is a barefoot runner who meditates, a skier who gets into the backcountry, a dad who is genuinely present. He does not just talk about balance and wellbeing — he lives it.

His curiosity extends into the kitchen, into nature, and into conversations that go somewhere real. He is the kind of person you could talk to for three hours and still feel there is more to discover.

He is based in Ruakaka, Northland, and works with clients across New Zealand and internationally via Zoom.
