Book

Time Management:
A Complete Waste of Time

The book that challenges everything you have been told about productivity, time and what it means to use your hours well.

About the Book

The productivity myth has been costing you everything

For decades, the self-help industry has sold you time management as the answer to feeling overwhelmed, unfulfilled and behind. Planners, apps, systems, hacks — all promising more hours, more control, more output.

Mikal Nielsen argues that every single one of them is missing the point. The problem is never time. It has always been mind.

In this book, Mikal dismantles the productivity myth with uncomfortable clarity and builds, in its place, a framework for understanding what actually determines how well you use the hours you have. It is not what you think. And once you see it, you cannot unsee it.

Inside the Book

What you will discover

Six core insights that reframe how you understand time, performance and what it means to live and work effectively.

Chapter Theme

Why time management fails everyone

The structural reason why every time management system eventually collapses — and why this is not a personal failing but a fundamental flaw in the approach itself.

Chapter Theme

The real currency: mental energy

You do not run out of time. You run out of mental energy. Learn what depletes it, what restores it and how to protect it fiercely.

Chapter Theme

The identity behind the busyness

Why many people are addicted to being busy — and the identity shift required to move from activity to genuine productivity and presence.

Chapter Theme

Decision fatigue and how to beat it

Every decision costs something. Mikal shows you how to restructure your mental environment so that your best decisions get your best mind.

Chapter Theme

Presence as performance

The ability to be fully present — at work, in relationships, in life — is not a luxury. It is the highest form of performance. Here is how to cultivate it.

Chapter Theme

A new framework for a full life

Moving beyond productivity into a model of meaningful engagement — where time well spent is defined not by output, but by alignment with what genuinely matters.
