Meet the Coach

Who's Mikal?

Earth citizen. Life coach. Author. Barefoot runner. Passionate about growth.

In Short

A guy who sits outside the box

If we ask his three children who Mikal is, the answer is simple: "Dad's weird!"

Most importantly, he sits outside of the box. He himself would say: "If anyone ever accuses me of being normal, I'm going to get very offended."

In short, he's a guy who is passionate about two things:

  • Growth — his own and facilitating it for others
  • Figuring out how things work — with a main focus on life and human beings

He tried a number of jobs and explored a couple of careers (banking and computers), but for the last 30 years he has followed his calling as a life coach — helping people get more life out of life, more business out of business, and more love out of relationships.

Background

From Denmark to New Zealand and everywhere in between

Mikal was born in Denmark but considers himself an earth citizen — not belonging to any country, culture, religion or political orientation. He has travelled to well over 50 countries, through which time he met his New Zealand wife.

He had a life-changing experience when he was 27, which paved the way for his passion for growth and facilitating it for others.

From a super shy, withdrawn and bullied child to a confident public speaker, presenter and coach — he has learned his craft in the real world, while also investing hugely in his own personal and business growth for more than 35 years.

Mikal has written four books (two published) and co-authored two with his wife (one published). He has appeared on national TV, national radio, and in most national newspapers in New Zealand.

"A revolution in thinking and approach to life, business and intimacy is desperately needed. I needed that in my life, and through a 35+ year journey of discovery, I have found the key." — Mikal Nielsen
Beyond the Coaching Room

The human behind the work

Other than coaching, Mikal is a husband, dad and café goer. He enjoys spending hours in the kitchen experimenting with food — to the delight or dismay of the family, depending on how the experiments go.

He is also a barefoot runner, just to add to the weirdness. And as you might guess from the photos, he likes outrunning his children on the ski slopes — although with his son now a ski instructor, it's the other way round.

His coaching is focussed on depth rather than width. If you have that feeling that there's got to be more to life than what you're experiencing, you are absolutely right — and Mikal can most likely help you discover more of your deepest potential.
